Frequently asked questions

  • How much does liposuction cost in NYC?

    Liposuction in NYC typically costs between $3,500 and $9,500 per treatment area for surgeon fees. Total cost depends on areas treated, technique (VASER and BodyTite cost more), and anesthesia type. A single area with local anesthesia may total $4,000–$7,000 all-in; multiple areas under general anesthesia can range from $15,000–$30,000+.

  • What factors affect the cost of liposuction?

    The main factors are: number of treatment areas, technique (VASER and BodyTite cost more than standard tumescent lipo), anesthesia type (local ~$500, general $2,500–$3,000), facility fees, and surgeon experience. In NYC, premium board-certified surgeons typically charge 20–40% more than the national average.

  • What is VASER liposuction and does it cost more?

    VASER uses ultrasound energy to liquefy fat before removal, allowing more precise contouring with less trauma to surrounding tissue. It typically costs $1,500–$2,500 more per area than standard lipo. Dr. Smith uses VASER for high-definition results and treatment of fibrous areas like the back and male chest.

  • What is BodyTite and is it worth the extra cost?

    BodyTite combines radiofrequency-assisted liposuction with simultaneous skin tightening — ideal for patients with moderate skin laxity who want to avoid a surgical lift. It costs approximately $2,000–$3,500 more per area. If skin tightening is not a concern, standard lipo or VASER may give comparable fat removal results at lower cost.

  • Are anesthesia and facility fees included in the estimate?

    No — the estimates above are surgeon fees only. Anesthesia and facility fees are additional: approximately $500 for a single area under local anesthesia, $1,000–$2,000 for 2–3 areas under twilight sedation, and $1,500–$3,000 for multiple areas under general anesthesia. Exact fees are confirmed at your consultation.

  • Is financing available for liposuction?

    Yes. Dr. Smith's practice offers financing through CareCredit and Alphaeon Credit, allowing patients to spread costs over 12–60 months. A $10,000 procedure, for example, could be financed from approximately $190–$280/month depending on the plan. Financing details are discussed at consultation.
